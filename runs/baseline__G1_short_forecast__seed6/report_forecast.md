## Methodology

Our forecasting approach combines three complementary methods to produce robust predictions:

### 1. **Baseline Method (Simple Average)**
- Projects the observed daily average forward
- Assumes stable business conditions
- Provides a conservative baseline estimate

### 2. **Trend-Adjusted Method**
- Incorporates observed growth patterns
- Applies a modest 2% monthly growth rate
- Accounts for business momentum and market expansion

### 3. **Seasonal-Adjusted Method**
- Applies typical retail seasonality patterns
- Key seasonal factors:
  - **December**: +15% (holiday shopping season)
  - **January**: -15% (post-holiday slowdown)
  - **February**: -10% (continued recovery)
  - **March**: Baseline (return to normal)
  - **April**: +5% (spring uptick)
  - **May**: +10% (continued growth)

### Final Forecast
The final forecast represents an **ensemble average** of the trend-adjusted and seasonal-adjusted methods, providing a balanced prediction that accounts for both growth momentum and seasonal patterns.

### Confidence Intervals
We provide ±15% confidence intervals around our forecasts to account for:
- Limited historical data (only 6 days)
- Market uncertainty
- Potential external factors
