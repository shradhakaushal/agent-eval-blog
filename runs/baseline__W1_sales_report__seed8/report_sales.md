# 2024 Sales Performance Report

## Executive Summary

This year, our business generated **$10.9 million in revenue**, with strong momentum heading into 2025. While we experienced some challenges mid-year, we finished the year on a high note with our best month ever in December.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Strong Year-End Recovery Drives Growth

After a challenging second quarter where revenue dipped to $866K in June (our lowest month), we rebounded strongly in the fourth quarter. **December was our best month of the year at $982K** — a 13% increase over June and 5% higher than November. This Q4 surge resulted in **3.8% growth compared to Q3**, demonstrating strong business momentum as we head into the new year.

The recovery was broad-based, but Electronics led the way with a 15% surge in December compared to our monthly average, showing particularly strong holiday season demand.

### 2. Electronics Dominates, Generating Half of All Revenue

Electronics is our clear revenue driver, accounting for **49% of total annual revenue ($5.3 million)**. This category consistently outperforms others, generating nearly twice the revenue of Apparel (30%) and more than double Home Goods (21%). 

However, all three categories maintained relatively stable pricing throughout the year (averaging $58-60 per unit), indicating that volume rather than price increases drove our revenue patterns.

### 3. Regional Performance Shows Clear Leader

The **West region is our strongest market**, generating 31% of total revenue ($3.4 million) — significantly ahead of our other regions. East (25%), South (24%), and North (21%) all performed reasonably well, but the West's leadership position suggests successful market penetration or favorable demographics that we should study and potentially replicate.

---

## Recommendation for Next Quarter

**Focus on sustaining the Q4 momentum by investigating and replicating what drove our December success.** 

Specifically, I recommend:

- **Conduct a deep-dive analysis** into what made December so successful (promotions, inventory levels, marketing campaigns, etc.) and identify which tactics can be sustained year-round
- **Invest in Electronics inventory and marketing** for Q1, as this category showed the strongest December performance (+15% vs. average) and represents our biggest revenue opportunity
- **Study the West region's success factors** and develop a playbook to improve performance in North, South, and East regions, which collectively represent 69% of our business but lag behind West's per-region performance

The mid-year slump (May-June) should also be addressed proactively. Consider launching targeted promotions or new product releases during these historically slower months to smooth out revenue fluctuations and maintain consistent growth throughout 2025.
