# 2024 Sales Performance Report

## Executive Summary

I'm pleased to report that 2024 was a solid year for our business, with **total revenue of $10.9 million** across all regions and product categories. While we faced some challenges mid-year, the business showed strong resilience and finished the year on a high note.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Strong Year-End Momentum Drove Overall Performance

December was our best month of the year, generating **$982,000 in revenue**—8% above our monthly average. This strong holiday performance helped us close out Q4 with $2.8 million in revenue, representing a 3.8% increase over Q3. The upward trend in the final quarter demonstrates that our business has positive momentum heading into 2025.

**What this means:** Our holiday season strategies worked well, and customer demand remains strong. The year-over-year trajectory is positive, with the second half of the year outperforming the first half by 2.2%.

### 2. Mid-Year Slowdown Required Course Correction

We experienced a noticeable dip in May and June, with June hitting our lowest monthly revenue of the year at $866,000. This represented a 4.4% decline from April levels. However, the business rebounded quickly in July with a 6.5% jump, showing our ability to adapt and recover.

**What this means:** While the mid-year slowdown was concerning, our quick recovery demonstrates operational resilience. Understanding what caused this dip will be important to prevent similar patterns in 2025.

### 3. Electronics Dominates, But Home Goods Shows Strongest Growth

Electronics remains our revenue powerhouse, accounting for **49% of total sales** ($5.3 million), followed by Apparel at 30% and Home Goods at 21%. However, Home Goods showed the strongest Q4 growth at 8.2%, outpacing both Electronics (1.9%) and Apparel (4.3%).

**What this means:** While we should continue to invest in our core Electronics business, the Home Goods category represents an emerging growth opportunity that deserves more attention and resources.

## Recommendation for Next Quarter

**Focus on sustaining momentum while addressing the Home Goods opportunity.**

Based on this year's performance, I recommend three priorities for Q1 2025:

1. **Capitalize on Home Goods Growth**: With 8.2% Q4 growth, this category is showing strong customer interest. Increase inventory, expand product selection, and consider targeted marketing campaigns to accelerate this trend.

2. **Prevent Another Mid-Year Dip**: Conduct a detailed analysis of what caused the May-June slowdown (seasonality, inventory issues, competition, etc.) and develop preventive strategies before we hit that period again.

3. **Replicate December's Success**: Document what worked in our holiday season approach—whether it was promotions, product mix, or marketing—and identify elements that can be applied to other months to smooth out revenue fluctuations.

The business is healthy and growing, but we have clear opportunities to improve consistency and accelerate growth in emerging categories.
