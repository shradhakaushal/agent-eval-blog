# 2024 Sales Performance Report

## Executive Summary

Our business delivered **$10.9 million in revenue** this year, with strong momentum building through the second half. While overall growth was modest at 2.2% from H1 to H2, the story beneath the surface reveals both significant opportunities and areas requiring immediate attention.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Electronics is Our Growth Engine – But We're Missing Opportunities Elsewhere

**Electronics revenue surged 18% in the second half of the year**, climbing from $2.0M in H1 to $2.9M in H2. This category now represents **49% of total company revenue** and is clearly resonating with customers. December was particularly strong, hitting $511K – our highest monthly Electronics revenue all year.

However, this success is masking a concerning trend: **Apparel and Home Goods both declined 11% in H2**. Apparel dropped from $1.7M to $1.5M, while Home Goods fell from $1.2M to $1.1M. These two categories together represent half our business, and their decline offset much of our Electronics gains.

### 2. Strong Holiday Finish Shows Customer Demand is There

December was our best month of the year at **$982K in revenue** – up 5.3% from November and 9% above our monthly average. Importantly, this wasn't just an Electronics story: all three categories grew in December, showing that when we execute well, customers respond across our entire product range.

The fourth quarter overall showed positive momentum, growing 3.8% over Q3, which suggests we're heading into next year with wind at our backs.

### 3. Regional Performance is Balanced, But West Leads

The **West region delivered $3.4M (31% of total revenue)**, outpacing all other regions. East, South, and North contributed $2.7M, $2.6M, and $2.2M respectively. Growth rates were relatively consistent across regions (1-4%), indicating our challenges and opportunities are product-related rather than geography-specific.

---

## Recommendation for Next Quarter

**Focus on revitalizing Apparel and Home Goods while maintaining Electronics momentum.**

Specifically, I recommend:

1. **Conduct customer research to understand why Apparel and Home Goods are declining.** Are we missing trends? Is it pricing? Product selection? We need answers before Q2.

2. **Apply our Electronics playbook to the struggling categories.** Whatever we're doing right in Electronics – whether it's marketing, product selection, or pricing – should be tested in Apparel and Home Goods.

3. **Capitalize on the December success.** All categories grew in December, proving demand exists. Analyze what worked differently that month (promotions, inventory, marketing) and replicate it in Q1.

If we can stabilize Apparel and Home Goods while maintaining Electronics growth, we're positioned for a strong 2025. But if the decline in two-thirds of our product categories continues, it will eventually drag down overall performance.
