# 2024 Sales Performance Report

## Executive Summary

Our business delivered solid performance in 2024, generating **$10.9 million in total revenue** and selling **185,244 units** across all regions and product categories. The year showed steady momentum with a strong finish in the fourth quarter.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Electronics is Our Growth Engine – Up 18% in Second Half

Electronics has emerged as our star performer, driving the majority of our revenue growth. In the second half of the year, Electronics revenue jumped **18.4%** compared to the first half, reaching over **$511,000 in December alone**. This category now represents **49% of total company revenue** ($5.3M of $10.9M).

**What this means:** Customer demand for Electronics is accelerating while our other categories are softening. This trend strengthened throughout the year, with Electronics showing consistent month-over-month gains from July through December.

### 2. Apparel and Home Goods Are Declining – Down 11% Each

While Electronics soared, our Apparel and Home Goods categories both declined approximately **11% in the second half** compared to the first half. Apparel revenue dropped from $1.7M (H1) to $1.5M (H2), and Home Goods fell from $1.2M (H1) to $1.1M (H2).

**What this means:** We're becoming increasingly dependent on a single category. These declines suggest either shifting customer preferences, increased competition, or potential issues with our product offerings in these categories.

### 3. Strong Holiday Season Momentum – Best Month of the Year

December was our strongest month, generating **$982,000 in revenue** – a **5.3% increase** over November and our highest monthly total all year. This strong finish helped us achieve **3.8% growth in Q4** compared to Q3, reversing a mid-year slowdown.

**What this means:** Our business has positive momentum heading into 2025. The holiday season proved we can execute well during peak demand periods, and customer engagement remains strong.

---

## Recommendation for Next Quarter

**Focus on revitalizing Apparel and Home Goods while maintaining Electronics momentum.**

Here's what to prioritize:

1. **Investigate the Apparel and Home Goods decline** – Conduct customer research to understand why these categories are underperforming. Are our products meeting customer needs? Is pricing competitive? Are there marketing gaps?

2. **Diversify our growth strategy** – While Electronics is thriving, relying on one category for growth is risky. Develop targeted campaigns to boost Apparel and Home Goods sales, potentially bundling them with Electronics purchases.

3. **Capitalize on Q4 momentum** – Analyze what worked in our holiday season execution and apply those lessons to Q1. Don't let the post-holiday period become a slump.

The business is healthy, but we need to address the category imbalance before it becomes a larger problem. Our strong December performance shows we have the operational capability to succeed – now we need to apply that across all product lines.
