# 2024 Sales Performance Report

## Executive Summary

I'm pleased to report that 2024 was a strong year for our business, with total revenue reaching **$10.9 million** across 185,000 units sold. The year finished on a high note, with December being our best month ever and momentum building into the new year.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Strong Year-End Momentum Driven by Electronics

Our business showed impressive acceleration in the final quarter, with **December revenue reaching $982,000—our highest monthly performance of the year**. This represents a 13% increase over our monthly average and was primarily driven by our Electronics category.

Electronics has emerged as our clear growth engine, showing a remarkable **24% increase from January to December**. This category now represents nearly half (49%) of our total revenue. In contrast, Apparel and Home Goods showed slight declines in the second half of the year, suggesting shifting customer preferences toward tech products.

### 2. Consistent Performance Across the Year with Steady Growth

Despite some mid-year softness (particularly in May-June), we maintained relatively stable monthly revenues throughout 2024, ranging from $866,000 to $982,000. The second half of the year outperformed the first half by 2.2%, demonstrating our ability to build momentum and finish strong.

Our fourth quarter was particularly impressive, growing 3.8% compared to Q3, with all regions contributing to this growth.

### 3. West Region Leads, But Opportunity Exists in North

The West region is our strongest performer, accounting for 31% of total revenue ($3.4 million), while the North region lags at 21% ($2.2 million). This represents a significant opportunity—if we could bring North region performance up to even the company average, we'd add approximately $500,000 in annual revenue.

---

## Recommendation for Next Quarter

**Focus on sustaining Electronics momentum while revitalizing Apparel sales.**

Given that Electronics is clearly driving our growth and customer demand remains strong, we should:

1. **Ensure adequate Electronics inventory** for Q1 2025 to capitalize on the momentum from December
2. **Investigate the Apparel decline** (down 5.5% year-over-year) and develop a targeted promotion or product refresh strategy for Q1
3. **Replicate West region best practices** in the North region to close the performance gap

The combination of our strong Electronics performance and the untapped potential in Apparel and the North region positions us well for continued growth in 2025.
