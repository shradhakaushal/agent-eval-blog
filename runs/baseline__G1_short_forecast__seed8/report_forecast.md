## Methodology

Given the limited historical data (6 days), we employed a conservative forecasting approach that combines multiple factors:

### 1. **Baseline Calculation**
- Calculated average daily revenue and units sold from available historical data
- Used these averages as the foundation for monthly projections

### 2. **Seasonal Adjustments**
Applied industry-standard seasonal factors to account for typical retail patterns:

| Month | Seasonal Factor | Rationale |
|-------|----------------|-----------|
| January | 0.85 (15% decrease) | Post-holiday slowdown, returns period |
| February | 0.90 (10% decrease) | Continued winter slowdown |
| March | 0.95 (5% decrease) | Gradual recovery |
| April | 1.00 (baseline) | Spring season begins |
| May | 1.05 (5% increase) | Spring peak |
| June | 1.10 (10% increase) | Summer season begins |

### 3. **Growth Trend**
- Applied a conservative 2% monthly growth rate
- Accounts for business expansion, marketing efforts, and market penetration
- Compounds month-over-month

### 4. **Calendar Adjustments**
- Adjusted for varying days per month (28-31 days)
- Ensures accurate monthly totals based on actual business days

### 5. **Formula**
```
Monthly Revenue = (Base Daily Revenue × Growth Factor × Seasonal Factor) × Days in Month

Where:
- Growth Factor = (1 + 0.02)^(months from baseline)
- Seasonal Factor = month-specific adjustment
- Days in Month = actual calendar days
```
