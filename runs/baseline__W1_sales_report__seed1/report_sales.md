# 2024 Sales Performance Report

## Executive Summary

Our business delivered **$10.9 million in revenue** for 2024, finishing the year with strong momentum. After a mid-year slowdown, we rebounded in the fourth quarter with three consecutive months of growth, culminating in our best month ever in December.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Strong Year-End Momentum Signals Recovery
After experiencing a dip in the second quarter (down 3.6% from Q1), our business has regained its footing. **The fourth quarter showed consistent growth**, with revenue increasing 3.8% compared to Q3. December was our strongest month of the year at **$982,000**, representing 8% above our monthly average. This upward trend across October, November, and December demonstrates that our business is heading into 2025 with positive momentum.

### 2. Electronics is Our Growth Engine
Electronics has emerged as our clear winner, accounting for **49% of total revenue** ($5.3 million). More importantly, Electronics grew **17% from Q1 to Q4**, while our other categories declined. In the fourth quarter alone, Electronics represented 52% of revenue, showing increasing customer preference for this category. The average price point across all categories remains consistent around $58-60, indicating stable pricing power.

### 3. Regional Performance Shows Clear Leader
The **West region outperformed all others**, generating $3.4 million (31% of total revenue). This is significantly ahead of our second-best region (East at 25%). The West region's strong performance suggests either better market conditions, more effective sales execution, or both—making it a model for other regions to study.

---

## Recommendation for Next Quarter

**Double down on Electronics in the West region while investigating the decline in Apparel and Home Goods.**

Specifically:
- **Invest in Electronics inventory and marketing** to capitalize on the clear customer demand trend
- **Study the West region's success** and replicate winning strategies in East, South, and North regions
- **Investigate why Apparel and Home Goods declined 9-11%** from Q1 to Q4—this represents lost opportunity that needs addressing before these categories erode further

The momentum we've built in Q4 positions us well for Q1 2025, but we need to act decisively to sustain growth across all product categories and regions.
