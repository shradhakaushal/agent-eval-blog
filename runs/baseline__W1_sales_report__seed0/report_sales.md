# 2024 Sales Performance Report

## Executive Summary

I'm pleased to report that 2024 was a solid year for our business, with **total revenue of $10.9 million** and a strong finish in the fourth quarter. While we faced some headwinds mid-year, our year-end performance demonstrates the resilience of our business model and the effectiveness of our product mix.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Strong Year-End Momentum Overcame Mid-Year Challenges

Our business showed remarkable resilience in 2024. After experiencing a slowdown in Q2 (down 3.6% from Q1), we rebounded strongly in the second half of the year. **December was our best month of the year at $982K in revenue**, representing a 5.3% increase over November and an 8.7% jump from our June low point of $866K.

The fourth quarter delivered **$2.81 million in revenue, up 3.8% from Q3**, demonstrating that our business has positive momentum heading into next year. This recovery was broad-based across all product categories, with Home Goods leading the charge with 8.2% growth in Q4.

### 2. Electronics Dominates, But All Categories Contribute

**Electronics remains our powerhouse category, generating 49% of total revenue** ($5.3 million) and selling nearly 90,000 units throughout the year. However, our diversified portfolio proved its value:

- **Electronics**: $5.3M (49% of revenue)
- **Apparel**: $3.2M (30% of revenue)  
- **Home Goods**: $2.3M (21% of revenue)

All three categories maintain similar average price points (around $58-60 per unit), indicating consistent value delivery across our product range. This diversification helped cushion the mid-year slowdown and contributed to our Q4 recovery.

### 3. Regional Performance Shows Clear Leader

The **West region is our strongest market, accounting for 31% of total revenue** ($3.4 million), significantly outperforming other regions. Our regional breakdown reveals opportunities for growth:

- **West**: $3.4M (31%)
- **East**: $2.7M (25%)
- **South**: $2.6M (24%)
- **North**: $2.2M (21%)

The West region's strong performance suggests we have winning strategies there that could potentially be replicated in underperforming regions like the North.

---

## Recommendation for Next Quarter

**Focus on replicating West region success in the North region while maintaining Q4 momentum.**

Given that the North region underperforms the West by nearly 52%, there's significant untapped potential. I recommend:

1. **Conduct a deep-dive analysis** of what's working in the West region (marketing strategies, product mix, pricing, distribution) and identify transferable best practices

2. **Invest in Home Goods inventory** for Q1, as this category showed the strongest Q4 growth (8.2%) and may have additional runway

3. **Maintain the momentum** from our strong December by ensuring adequate inventory levels across all categories to meet early-year demand

By closing even half the gap between North and West region performance, we could add approximately $580K in annual revenue—a 5% boost to our top line.
