# 2024 Sales Performance Report

## Executive Summary

This year delivered solid results with **$10.9 million in total revenue** across all regions and product categories. While we maintained steady performance throughout most of the year, the business showed strong momentum in the final quarter, setting us up well for 2025.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Strong Year-End Momentum Signals Growing Demand

December was our best month of the year, generating **$982K in revenue**—8% above our monthly average. This wasn't a one-month fluke: we saw consistent acceleration throughout Q4, with each month performing better than the last. This upward trend suggests our recent initiatives are gaining traction with customers.

**The numbers:**
- Q4 total revenue: $2.8M
- December alone: $982K (our highest month)
- November: $932K
- October: $893K

### 2. Electronics is Our Growth Engine

Electronics drove nearly half of our total revenue (49%) and was the only category showing strong growth. While Apparel and Home Goods both declined by about 10% from Q1 to Q4, **Electronics grew by 17%** over the same period.

**Category breakdown for 2024:**
- **Electronics:** $5.3M (49% of revenue) ✓ Growing
- **Apparel:** $3.2M (30% of revenue) ↓ Declining
- **Home Goods:** $2.3M (21% of revenue) ↓ Declining

The average price point across categories is similar ($57-60), so this isn't about pricing—customers are simply choosing Electronics more often.

### 3. Regional Performance Shows Clear Leaders

The West region led all territories with **$3.4M in revenue (31% of total)**, while the North region lagged at $2.2M (21%). This 55% gap between our best and worst performing regions represents a significant opportunity.

**Regional rankings:**
1. West: $3.4M (31%)
2. East: $2.7M (25%)
3. South: $2.6M (23%)
4. North: $2.2M (21%)

## Recommendation for Next Quarter

**Focus on expanding Electronics inventory and marketing while investigating what's working in the West region.**

Here's why this matters:

1. **Double down on Electronics:** This category is clearly resonating with customers and growing while others decline. Ensure we have adequate inventory, consider expanding our Electronics product line, and allocate more marketing budget to this category.

2. **Learn from the West:** Our West region is outperforming others by a significant margin. Conduct a deep dive to understand what's different—is it the sales team, local marketing, product mix, or customer demographics? Apply those learnings to boost performance in other regions, especially the North.

3. **Address the Apparel and Home Goods decline:** While Electronics should be our priority, we can't ignore the 10% decline in our other categories. Consider refreshing product offerings, running targeted promotions, or investigating whether customer preferences have shifted.

The strong December finish shows we have momentum. By focusing our resources on what's working (Electronics and the West region playbook), we can accelerate growth in Q1 2025.
