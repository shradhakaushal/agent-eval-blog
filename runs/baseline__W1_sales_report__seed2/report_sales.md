# 2024 Sales Performance Report

## Executive Summary

The business delivered **$10.9 million in revenue** for 2024, finishing the year on a strong note with our best month in December. While overall performance remained relatively stable throughout the year, a closer look reveals important shifts in our business that require attention.

---

## Top 3 Key Findings

### 1. **Strong Finish to the Year, Led by December Performance**

We ended 2024 with momentum, generating **$982K in December**—our highest revenue month of the year and **8% above our monthly average**. Fourth quarter revenue of $2.8 million exceeded Q1 by 2.2%, demonstrating positive year-end trends. This strong finish was particularly encouraging given the relatively flat performance we saw mid-year.

### 2. **Electronics is Driving Growth While Apparel is Declining**

Our product mix is shifting significantly:

- **Electronics** has become our growth engine, representing **49% of total revenue** ($5.3M) and growing **17% from Q1 to Q4**. This category showed consistent strength, particularly in the second half of the year.

- **Apparel**, our second-largest category at 30% of revenue ($3.2M), is trending in the wrong direction with a **9.5% decline from Q1 to Q4**. Sales peaked in March and have steadily decreased since then.

- **Home Goods** (21% of revenue) also declined 11% from Q1 to Q4, suggesting challenges in our non-electronics categories.

### 3. **West Region Leads, But Growth is Uneven Across Markets**

The **West region is our strongest market**, accounting for **31% of total revenue** ($3.4M), followed by East (25%), South (23%), and North (21%). However, the relatively even distribution suggests we haven't fully capitalized on regional opportunities, and there may be untapped potential in our smaller markets.

---

## Recommendation for Next Quarter

**Focus on reversing the Apparel decline while maintaining Electronics momentum.**

Specifically, we should:

1. **Investigate the Apparel downturn immediately** - Conduct customer research to understand why Apparel sales have dropped 9.5%. Is this a pricing issue, product selection problem, or changing customer preferences? The steady decline since March suggests a systematic issue that needs addressing.

2. **Double down on Electronics success** - Analyze what's working in Electronics (product mix, pricing, marketing) and apply those lessons to other categories. Consider expanding our Electronics inventory and marketing investment given the strong growth trajectory.

3. **Develop a regional growth strategy** - With the West region significantly outperforming others, study what makes this market successful and create a playbook to replicate that success in North, South, and East regions.

The good news is we're ending the year strong. The challenge is ensuring we address the category imbalances before they impact overall growth in 2025.
